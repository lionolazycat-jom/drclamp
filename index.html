Product Overview

What Is the
Dr. B™ Clamp?

The Dr. B™ Clamp is a sterile, single-use circumcision device developed by Dr. Hasbi, a specialist circumcision surgeon. Engineered as a two-part assembly, it fits securely over the glans to enable a controlled, haemostatic excision of the prepuce.

Unlike conventional methods, the Dr. B™ Clamp reduces operative variability by standardising tissue compression and excision angle — delivering a reproducible, aesthetically consistent outcome across all patient demographics.

Three precision-engineered locking clips apply differential circumferential compression — providing superior tissue control, improved haemostasis, and a more predictable excision line.

The elongated inner tube geometry ensures atraumatic positioning against the glans, enabling secure retention throughout the procedure and facilitating painless clamp removal post-operatively.

The precisely calibrated 45° oblique cutting plane mirrors the natural glans corona anatomy, ensuring the wound margin aligns with physiological tissue planes for a superior cosmetic result.

Clinical Q&A

Frequently Asked
Questions

Common queries from healthcare professionals and patients regarding the Dr. B™ Clamp procedure, post-operative care, and recovery.

Is the procedure painful for the patient?
The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia, which typically provides effective analgesia for 2–3 hours. Appropriate oral analgesia is prescribed to manage discomfort during the subsequent recovery period.
What does post-procedure care involve?
Post-operative management is straightforward. Patients may shower from the day following the procedure. The clamp should be gently rinsed with warm water after urination and during bathing.
How is the clamp removed?
The clamp may be removed at the clinic by the attending practitioner, or by the patient or caregiver at home using a standard nail clipper or small cutter. Written removal instructions are provided at discharge.
What is the expected healing timeline?
Wound healing is typically expected within 1–2 weeks post-procedure. Recovery duration may vary depending on the patient's age, anatomical factors, and individual healing response.
When should the clamp be removed?
The optimal removal window is typically between 2 and 6 days post-procedure, depending on the patient's age and the clinical condition of the tissue at the time of review.
What preparation is required before clamp removal?
Prior to removal, the patient should soak the clamp site in warm water for approximately 30 minutes. This softens any desiccated tissue around the clamp interface, facilitating atraumatic and comfortable removal.
